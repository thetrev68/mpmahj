# UI/UX Audit — 2026-03-22 (Dark Mode, Charleston Phase)

Captured from screenshot: localhost:5173, dark mode, 1st Charleston Pass Right (Pass 1 of 3).

---

## Critical Layout Issues

### 1. Mahjong button is prominent and pulsing during Charleston

**Files:** [ActionBarPhaseActions.tsx:121-137](apps/client/src/components/game/ActionBarPhaseActions.tsx#L121-L137), [CharlestonPhase.tsx:167-170](apps/client/src/components/game/phases/CharlestonPhase.tsx#L167-L170)

**Problem:** During Charleston, `canDeclareMahjong` is `true` whenever the player holds 14 tiles and isn't dead — which is almost always true during Charleston. The `charlestonDemoted` flag at line 162 is `!canDeclareMahjong`, so it's `false`, meaning the button renders with the full gold gradient + `motion-safe:animate-pulse` animation. A flashing gold "Mahjong" button during tile-passing is confusing and distracting.

**Expected:** During Charleston, the Mahjong button should be demoted to an outline style with no animation. Declaring Mahjong during Charleston is an edge case — it shouldn't compete visually with the Proceed button.

### 2. Staging area and action pane are not treated as one fixed board-region unit

**Files:** [CharlestonPhase.tsx:408-527](apps/client/src/components/game/phases/CharlestonPhase.tsx#L408-L527), [PlayerZone.tsx:19-21](apps/client/src/components/game/PlayerZone.tsx#L19-L21)

**Problem:** The staging area and action pane currently behave like adjacent layout fragments rather than one fixed board-region component. The Charleston board uses a 3×3 CSS grid (`grid-cols-[auto_1fr_auto] grid-rows-[auto_1fr_auto]`). The player zone is at `row-start-3 col-span-3 self-end` with `z-20`, while the right opponent rack is at `col-start-3 row-start-2`. Because the PlayerZone extends the full width via `col-span-3`, it overlaps the bottom portion of the side opponent racks and crowds the south (right-side on screen) opponent.

The ActionBar's fixed `min-w-[180px]` width and the PlayerZone grid's `lg:grid-cols-[minmax(0,1fr)_280px]` force a wide action area that can push into opponent space, but the larger issue is structural: the staging outlines and the Proceed/action controls are not locked into one stable container with fixed boundaries. That makes the interactive region feel like it shifts around instead of reading as one permanent "look here" zone.

**Expected:** The staging area and action pane should be rendered as a single outer container bounded by:

- left: inside edge of the north/west opponent rack
- top: bottom edge of the discard pile
- right: inside edge of the south/east opponent rack
- bottom: top edge of the player rack

Inside that container, the staging tile outlines must have fixed anchor positions from the start of the game and remain in those positions throughout Charleston and normal gameplay. The number of visible outlines may change by state, but their origin/placement should not jump. The Proceed button and any sibling action buttons must also remain in a fixed location for the duration of the game so players always know where to look.

### 3. Side opponent racks are inset from the board perimeter

**Files:** [CharlestonPhase.tsx:402-406](apps/client/src/components/game/phases/CharlestonPhase.tsx#L402-L406), [OpponentRack.tsx:47](apps/client/src/components/game/OpponentRack.tsx#L47)

**Problem:** The side opponent racks are being positioned as centered elements inside the board grid instead of being constrained to the outer perimeter of the playable board. The opponent rack has a fixed span of `OPPONENT_RACK_SPAN_PX = 32*16 + 2*15 = 542px`, but there is no layout rule that ties its outer edges to the same bounding square as the player rack. As a result, the north/west and south/east side racks sit inset from the board edges, which shrinks the usable center area and makes the discard field feel cramped.

**Expected:** Treat the playable board as a perfect square and place all four racks on that square's perimeter. The outermost left edge of the north/west side rack should line up with the outermost left edge of the player rack, and the outermost right edge of the south/east side rack should line up with the outermost right edge of the player rack. The player rack can remain visually larger, but all four racks should define the same outside square so the center of the board is maximized for the discard pile.

### 4. Player rack overlaps / bleeds into the right rail

**Files:** [GameBoard.tsx:403-406](apps/client/src/components/game/GameBoard.tsx#L403-L406)

**Problem:** The outer layout is `flex` with the square board container using `lg:w-[min(1200px,calc(100vh-5rem),calc(100vw-25rem))]` and the right rail using `lg:min-w-[24rem] lg:flex-1`. But the Charleston phase's internal grid (`absolute inset-0`) positions the player zone at the bottom spanning the full width of the square-board-container. On viewports where the board is wide, the player rack tiles extend beyond the board boundary and visually overlap the right rail's left edge. The `overflow-hidden` on the right rail clips its own content but doesn't prevent the board content from overlapping.

**Expected:** The player rack must be constrained within the square board boundary. Either the rack needs `overflow-hidden` or the board container needs it, or the rack width needs to be capped.

### 5. Leave Game button overlaps Log Out button

**Files:** [GameBoard.tsx:353-383](apps/client/src/components/game/GameBoard.tsx#L353-L383)

**Problem:** Both buttons are in a `flex items-center gap-2` container positioned `absolute right-4 top-4 z-40 lg:right-0`. On large screens where the right rail is visible, `lg:right-0` pushes the buttons flush against the right edge of the square board container (which is `relative`). But the right rail sits to the right of the board — so the buttons can appear to overlap or crowd the right rail's top-left corner. In the screenshot, "Leave Game" visually overlaps/crowds "Log Out".

**Expected:** The control strip should account for the right rail and not be pushed to the very edge, or should be repositioned above the board in a non-overlapping header area.

## Right Rail Issues

### 6. Large empty green area above "AI HINT" in right rail

**Files:** [GameBoard.tsx:453-457](apps/client/src/components/game/GameBoard.tsx#L453-L457)

**Problem:** The right rail has a top section (`right-rail-top`) styled as `flex-1 bg-muted/20` that acts as a spacer. When the hint section doesn't need extra vertical space (`hintNeedsExtraVerticalSpace = false`), both top and bottom sections have `flexGrow: 1`, splitting the rail ~50/50. The top half is completely empty — just a tinted background. This wastes half the rail on nothing.

**Expected:** Either use the top section for something useful (game info, player stats, discard pile summary, pattern reference) or collapse it so the hint panel gets the full height.

### 7. Right rail theme mismatch in dark mode

**Files:** [GameBoard.tsx:450](apps/client/src/components/game/GameBoard.tsx#L450), [GameBoard.tsx:459](apps/client/src/components/game/GameBoard.tsx#L459)

**Problem:** The right rail uses `lg:bg-card/92 lg:backdrop-blur-md` and the bottom section uses `bg-background/35 dark:bg-muted/50`. In the screenshot, the top section renders as a slightly lighter green-ish area (the board's green bleeding through at 20% opacity). The overall feel isn't "dark mode" — it's translucent green. US-080 was marked completed but the surfaces still don't feel like true dark surfaces.

**Expected:** Dark-mode right rail should use opaque dark surfaces (e.g., `dark:bg-zinc-900` or similar) rather than semi-transparent overlays on the green board.

## ActionBar / Interaction Issues

### 8. ActionBar instruction text may be cut off

**Files:** [ActionBar.tsx:108-113](apps/client/src/components/game/ActionBar.tsx#L108-L113)

**Problem:** The instruction copy is living inside a pane whose size and relationship to the staging area are not clearly fixed. The text "Charleston. Select 3 tiles to pass right, then press Proceed." is long enough that it can truncate or wrap awkwardly within the current 280px action slot. If the text block grows or reflows unpredictably, it risks pushing nearby controls or making the action area feel unstable from state to state.

**Expected:** Instruction text can change dynamically during play, but it must stay inside the fixed staging/action container and must not move the Proceed button, shift the action controls, or expand into neighboring board space. The text region should be treated as flexible content inside a fixed action sub-panel, not as something that redefines the layout.

### 9. "0/3 selected" counter placement

**Screenshot observation:** The "0/3 selected" text appears below the player rack, outside the board area. That breaks the idea of a single fixed interaction region for staging + actions and makes the selection state feel detached from where the player is supposed to act.

**Expected:** The selection counter should live inside the same fixed staging/action container as the tile outlines and Proceed button. It can be visually associated with either the staging side or the action side, but it should not sit outside that shared container or appear to float relative to the rack.

## Opponent Rack Issues

### 10. Opponent racks have no spatial relationship to the board edges

**Files:** [CharlestonPhase.tsx:411](apps/client/src/components/game/phases/CharlestonPhase.tsx#L411)

**Problem:** The grid uses `gap-x-3 px-3` (12px gap + padding) which is tight. The opponent racks float with `auto` sizing without any min-height constraint on the center row, so the "playing field" area between racks feels cramped.

### 11. Side opponent rack wood texture may not fit dark mode

**Files:** [OpponentRack.tsx:92-96](apps/client/src/components/game/OpponentRack.tsx#L92-L96), [rackStyles.ts](apps/client/src/components/game/rackStyles.ts) (if exists)

**Problem:** The `RACK_WOOD_STYLE` is applied identically in both light and dark mode. Wood textures that look natural in light mode can look jarring against a dark background.

## Z-Index Stacking Issues

### 12. Multiple competing z-index layers

**Observed z-indices:**

- `z-40`: board controls strip (Leave Game / Log Out)
- `z-50`: leave-loading overlay
- `z-20`: PlayerZone
- `z-20`: error message (line 548)

## Player Rack Issues

### 13. Rack width not constrained to board

The player rack renders all 14+ tiles at their natural size. On wider boards, the rack can exceed the intended board width. There's no `max-width` or `overflow-hidden` on the rack slot to prevent bleed.

### 14. Rack background extends beyond board in screenshot

In the screenshot, the brown wood-textured rack background visibly extends under/behind the right rail, suggesting the rack's container is wider than the board's logical boundary.

## General Dark Mode Theme Issues

### 15. Board-level green felt background bleeds through transparent layers

The semi-transparent backgrounds (`bg-card/92`, `bg-muted/20`, `bg-background/35`) let the green board background bleed through, making everything look green-tinted in dark mode rather than true dark surfaces.

### 16. Instruction text contrast

The instruction text "Charleston. Select 3 tiles..." uses `text-muted-foreground` styling in dark mode. In the screenshot, it appears as light text on a semi-transparent dark background, which may have contrast issues depending on what's behind it.

## Fixed Positioning Issues

### 19. TurnIndicator uses viewport-percentage fixed positioning — breaks with right rail

**Files:** [TurnIndicator.tsx:57-62](apps/client/src/components/game/TurnIndicator.tsx#L57-L62)

**Problem:** Turn indicator positions use `fixed` with viewport-percentage offsets (e.g., `right-[8%]` for East). With the right rail present at `lg` breakpoints, `right-[8%]` on a 1440px viewport = 115px from viewport edge, which lands inside the 384px-wide right rail — not on the board. The indicators are positioned relative to the full viewport, not the board container.

**Expected:** Indicators should be positioned relative to the square board container, not the viewport.

### 20. Fixed headers can collide at viewport top

**Files:** [CharlestonTracker.tsx](apps/client/src/components/game/CharlestonTracker.tsx), [WallCounter.tsx](apps/client/src/components/game/WallCounter.tsx)

**Problem:** CharlestonTracker uses `fixed top-0 left-0 right-0 z-20`. WallCounter uses `fixed top-[60px] left-4 z-10`. If the tracker wraps to more than 60px tall (bot messages, readiness indicators), it overlaps the WallCounter. All top-area offsets are hardcoded pixels with no dynamic awareness of sibling heights.

### 21. Fixed 1038px player rack causes horizontal scroll on mid-width viewports

**Files:** [PlayerRack.tsx:176](apps/client/src/components/game/PlayerRack.tsx#L176)

**Problem:** Player rack uses `style={{ width: '1038px' }}` (hardcoded `PLAYER_RACK_SPAN_PX = 63*16 + 2*15`). On viewports between ~1024px and ~1070px, the rack needs 1038px but only has `100vw - 32px` available (due to `px-4`). No `overflow-x: hidden` is set on any ancestor, producing a horizontal scrollbar.

## Responsive / Layout Math Issues

### 17. Board width formula may not account for all padding

**Files:** [GameBoard.tsx:406](apps/client/src/components/game/GameBoard.tsx#L406)

Formula: `lg:w-[min(1200px,calc(100vh-5rem),calc(100vw-25rem))]`

The outer container has `px-4 lg:pr-0` (so 16px left padding only on lg). The 25rem accounts for 24rem rail + 1rem, but the `px-4` left padding (16px = 1rem) makes the total deducted space 26rem, not 25rem. This 1rem discrepancy can cause the layout to be slightly wider than viewport on some screen sizes.

### 18. Right rail `lg:pr-0` removes right padding from layout

**Files:** [GameBoard.tsx:403](apps/client/src/components/game/GameBoard.tsx#L403)

The outer `flex` container has `px-4 pb-4 pt-16 lg:pr-0`. Removing right padding on large screens means the right rail goes edge-to-edge against the viewport, with no breathing room. This looks intentional but may contribute to the cramped feel.
